Home Energy Monitor

May 11, 2026

A clamp-on power meter and dashboard for tracking household electricity use in real time.

A DIY whole-home energy monitor built to make electricity use visible — because you can't reduce what you can't see.

The idea

Most people have no real-time sense of how much power their home draws. I wanted a low-cost way to measure it at the panel and surface the data in a simple dashboard.

How it works

  • Sensing. Split-core current transformers clamp around the mains feeds — no rewiring required.
  • Acquisition. An ESP32 samples the CT outputs, computes RMS current and real power, and publishes readings over MQTT.
  • Dashboard. A small web app charts live load, daily kWh, and estimated cost, with a focus on spotting waste.

What I learned

Calibrating the CTs and handling the analog front-end cleanly was the hard part. Once the data was trustworthy, the behavior change was immediate — phantom loads and an aging fridge were easy to spot and fix.

Next steps

  • Per-circuit monitoring
  • Solar generation overlay
  • Automated alerts for unusual consumption
